Cybercrime Case Study
One common cybercrime is phishing, where attackers send fake emails or messages to trick users into sharing personal information.
These messages often appear to come from trusted sources such as banks or organizations.
For example, a student may receive an email claiming to be from a bank, asking them to update their account details through a link.
If the student clicks the link and enters their information, the attacker can steal the data and misuse it.
To prevent such cybercrimes, users should avoid clicking on suspicious links, verify the sender, and never share sensitive information online.
Awareness and caution are the best ways to stay safe.
